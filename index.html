Five rules that actually work
01
50–30–20
50% on needs (rent, food, transport), 30% on wants, 20% saved. The savings 20% is non-negotiable — pay yourself before paying everyone else.
02
Emergency fund first
Save 3 months of expenses somewhere safe before investing. This stops you from taking a 24% personal loan every time something goes wrong.
03
Good debt vs bad debt
A home loan builds an asset. A personal loan for a phone or wedding function builds nothing. Ask: will this be worth more than I pay back?
04
Start a SIP today
₹500/month at 12% for 20 years = ₹49,000. Same money in savings = ₹13,200. The gap is time — and you can't buy time back.
05
Social pressure costs real money
A ₹15,000 phone on EMI at 18% costs ₹17,600 total. The next model is out in 12 months. Spending to impress is one of the biggest wealth destroyers for young Indians.
The debt trap — how it closes
Month 1: One EMI. Tiny monthly amount. Barely noticeable.
Month 8: Second need. Another loan. Two EMIs now. Still fine.
Month 18: Three EMIs = 40% of income. No savings. Any surprise = panic.
Month 24: Emergency hits. Personal loan at 24%. The trap closes.
Alternative path: ₹2,000/month saved from day one → ₹48,000 in 24 months. Emergency handled. No trap. No panic.
